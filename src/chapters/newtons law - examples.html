Newton's Laws Of Motion

DIRECTION OF MOTION POSITIVE

Horizontal
The vertical resultant = 0 N.
The horizontal resultant determines acceleration.
Pulled at an angle

Free-body
Horizontal:
Fnet = ma
FAx + (−Ff) = ma
Vertical:
Fnet = 0
(−Fg) + FN + FAy = 0

Pushed at an angle

Free-body
Horizontal:
Fnet = ma
FAx + (−Ff) = ma
Vertical:
Fnet = 0
Fg + (−FN) + FAy = 0

Slopes
The perpendicular (⟂) resultant = 0 N.
The parallel (//) resultant determines acceleration
REMEMBER: Use components of weight.
Fg// = Fg sin θ
Fg⊥ = Fg cos θ
Force applied down the slope

Free-body
Parallel:
Fnet = ma
Fg// + FAx + (−Ff) = ma
Perpendicular:
Fnet = 0
Fg⊥+ (−FN) = 0
Force applied up the slope

Free-body
Parallel:
Fnet = ma
(-Fg//) + FAx + (−Ff) = ma
Perpendicular:
Fnet = 0
Fg⊥+ (−FN) = 0
No force applied

Free-body
Parallel:
Fnet = ma
Fg// + (−Ff) = ma
Perpendicular:
Fnet = 0
Fg⊥+ (−FN) = 0

Suspended
Horizontal resultant = 0 N.
Vertical resultant determines acceleration.
REMEMBER: No normal or friction forces.
Lift stationary/constant velocity
Diagram and free-body diagram
Vertical:
Fnet = 0
Fg + (-FT) = 0
Lift stationary/constant velocity
Diagram and free-body diagram
Vertical:
Fnet = ma
Fg + (-FT) = ma
Acceleration will be in the direction of the greatest force.
Lift in freefall (cable snap)
Diagram and free-body diagram
Vertical:
Fnet = ma
Fg + (-FT) = ma

Connected objects

Do separate free body diagrams for each object.

The velocity and acceleration of all objects are equal in magnitude and direction.

Applied forces are applied to only one object at a time.

Simultaneous equations for acceleration and tension are sometimes needed.

REMEMBER:
Ropes/cables- The tension forces on the objects are the same in magnitude but opposite in direction.
Touching objects- Newton’s Third Law

Same axis
Can be horizontal (multiple objects on a surface) or vertical (multiple suspended objects).

The velocity and acceleration of all objects are equal in magnitude and direction.

Multiple axes
Horizontal (objects on a surface) AND vertical (suspended objects).

The velocity and acceleration of all objects are equal in magnitude NOT DIRECTION.

Vector direction on multiple axes
Clockwise:
Right and Down positive
Left and Up negative

Anti-clockwise:
Left and Up positive
Right and Down negative
